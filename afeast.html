A Feast

You drove up the car to the site and you have a cooler full of everything you could possibly need for a large meal. This recipe is designed to please a very large crowd that is eager for a good meal. I tried to go with a recipe that can be adapted in almost any circumstance. Even if you aren’t in the bush, this is still a great meal to whip up at home.


Chicken Fried Rice

It doesn’t need to be chicken, it can be steak, tofu, fish, even just plain old egg. Fried rice is a staple in, not just my camping diet but my weekly diet as well. I love to use brown rice to keep it healthy. But if white rice is your thing, go for it friend. Use a wok if you can, if not, just a 12-inch frying pan with big slopping sides will do.

  • One chicken breast, cut into large chunks
  • Two cups cooked rice
  • One clove minced garlic
  • Half a red onion
  • Two carrots chopped
  • A third cup peas
  • A quarter cup cpring onion
  • Soy sauce
  • Sesame oil

  1. Crack 2 eggs. Beat it well.
  2. Heat up the wok. Add about 1 tbsp of vegetable oil. Give it a toss, so the bottom is coated nicely.
  3. Wait until there is smoke coming out. Pour in the egg. Will take about 30-50 seconds to get it fluffy. Break it into small pieces and set it aside.
  4. Add more oil into the same wok. Heat it up and add in the chicken. Saute it about 1 or 2 minutes. Then push it to the side so you have room for the vegetables. Dump in all your veggies.
  5. When the onions turn clear add salt to taste. Clear out pan.
  6. Heat up the wok for about 15 seconds. Then add 2 tbsp of vegetable oil. Put in the rice right after that. Use your spatula to break down the big pieces.
  7. Stir fry for two minutes then add seasonings, soy sauce and sesame oil.
  8. Add in rest of ingredients to heat back up.
